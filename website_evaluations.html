ITIS3135 Website Evaluations

CRAP Website Evaluations

Zillow

Zillow is a real estate website for people to purchase real estate through their services. It's very straightforward.

  • Contrast: While I find the website particularly bright, it is easy to read all text, and elements that need to stand out properly do, with great use of colors throughout.
  • Repetition: This is a great example of good repetition. The tonal and artistic similarity across the whole page leads to a cohesive, organized vision.
  • Alignment: All the elements of this page are properly aligned in such a way to draw connection as well as lead to cohesion and an organized feeling while browsing.
  • Proximity: Groups of related links, represented as large buttons, are grouped together to make finding the right option easier for the user.

Pacific Northwest X-Ray Inc.

Pacific Northwest X-Ray Inc. is the website for sales and marketing of the titular company, who provide X-Ray services.

  • Contrast: This is one thing the site actually does well, but it may be a bit too much contrast. The colors differ to the point of eye strain.
  • Repetition: This is not great here. All of the site's assets get used for a single part and then abandoned for a new graphic. There is little to no cohesion between design elements.
  • Alignment: From the moment you open the page, you'll notice how much this site fails at alignment. The row of text with "Search for X-Ray Products" has an arrow that fails to correctly point to the search bar.
  • Proximity: This one is a bit hard to judge, as there isn't much to this site beyond a single purpose. However, one could argue that the grouping together of every single service provided by the company in a list is inappropriate use of proximity.
